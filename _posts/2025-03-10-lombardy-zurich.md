### The Hostel
The Yellowsquare Milan Hostel was the cleanest and most comfortable hostel I've stayed at, and I expect nothing less given its high price. They were running a trivia night and I had a free drink voucher to use, so I got a beer and looked for a team. I heard some chatter in Indian English and turned to find three guys sitting at a table for four. I said hi and they welcomed me in! One guy was a North Indian working at CERN, who met the other two at the hostel. He had just come from Lausanne a few days before me!

The other two were doctoral students at Augberg. When I said I'm from Canada, one replied, "I'm also Kannada!" He's from Bengalore ü§£. The other student was Chinese (perhaps he said from the North?). They were both just beginning to learn German, so they communicated in English with their heavy accents. I had no problem understanding either of them, but it was funny to watch them unable to understand each other from time to time ü§≠. While the Indians were in the toilet, the Chinese guy spoke to me in Mandarin. He was curious to know if I considered myself to be Chinese or Canadian.
